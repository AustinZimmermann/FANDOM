## Overview

In a world where mutants (evolved humans with superhuman abilities) exist and face widespread prejudice, two distinct factions prepare for an inevitable clash: the supremacist Brotherhood led by Magneto, and the pacifist X-Men led by Professor Charles Xavier. This pivotal 2000s superhero film helped kickstart the modern comic book cinematic era.

The 2009 Blu-ray upgrade marked a significant visual leap from the previous DVD releases, offering a high-definition 1080p presentation alongside a suite of special features. ## Special Features

This set carried over popular extras from the DVD era, retrofitted with interactive viewing modes designed for early Blu-ray players.

**Disc 1: Feature Film & Extras**

* **Audio Commentary:** Featuring director Bryan Singer and Brian Peck.
* **Enhanced Viewing Mode:** An interactive track allowing viewers to access behind-the-scenes footage and deleted/extended scenes seamlessly branched into the film.
* **Deleted/Extended Scenes:** Six scenes (approximately 11 minutes total) with optional commentary by Bryan Singer and Brian Peck.
* **Fox Special: “The Mutant Watch”:** A 22-minute mock TV news broadcast starring Bruce Davison as Senator Kelly, detailing the in-universe mutant controversy mixed with promotional behind-the-scenes footage.
